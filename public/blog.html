Benefits of Grass-Fed Beef Tallow

Published August 2025

Tallow, or rendered animal fat, is made by melting raw fat (also called suet) until it is liquid. Beef tallow (rendered beef suet) has been used in cuisine, cosmetics, and health products for centuries.

Why tallow loves your skin

  • Omega-3 & Omega-6 fatty acids — anti-inflammatory support that can help heal and prevent eczema & rosacea.
  • Non-comedogenic — won’t clog pores and supports collagen production.
  • Fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K & B12 — a uniquely animal-based bundle of nutrients that deeply nourishes the skin.
  • Stearic & oleic acids — also found in our skin’s outer layer and sebum; softening, moisturizing, regenerative, and anti-inflammatory.
  • Palmitoleic & palmitic acids — compounds our skin loses with age that help maintain a youthful look.
Bottom line: Grass-fed beef tallow doesn’t just moisturize (you’ll feel it immediately) — it helps heal, restore, protect, and regenerate the skin’s natural barrier.
